For each positive integer $n$, let $S(n)$ be the sum of the digits in the decimal expansion of $n$. Prove that for all $n$,

$$S(2n) \leq 2S(n) \leq 10S(2n)$$

and show that there exists $n$ such that $S(n) = 1996S(3n)$.
